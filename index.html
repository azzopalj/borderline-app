How it works

Simple enough for casual fans.
Deep enough for diehards.

1

A pitch is thrown

Watch the ball fly from the pitcher's hand toward the plate in real perspective. The ball's speed, break, and trajectory are all derived from real Statcast measurements.

2

You make the call

Before the zone is revealed, tap Ball or Strike. The measurement bar showing the batter's knee and letter heights fades out as the ball arrives — no cheating.

3

See where it actually landed

The rulebook strike zone appears with the ball at its exact coordinates. Borderline pitches earn bonus points. You'll also see whether the actual umpire got it right.

4

Beat the ump

At the end of each game, your accuracy is compared directly against the umpire's. Some MLB umps sit around 94% accuracy. Can you beat them?
